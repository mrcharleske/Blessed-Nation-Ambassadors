Who We Are

Lydia Waweru founded Blessed Nation Ambassadors in 2017 after experiencing poverty and hardship herself. Her story of resilience became the foundation for an organization committed to serving orphans, widows, street families, children living with disabilities, youth, farmers, and small business owners.

We focus on feeding programs, social support, mentorship, and academic sponsorship for children living with disabilities, ensuring they have the opportunity to learn and thrive.

“Religion that is pure and undefiled before God and the Father is this: to visit orphans and widows in their affliction, and to keep oneself unstained from the world.” — James 1:27

What We Do

Visiting the Less Fortunate

Regular visits and emotional support to orphans, widows, and street families — we bring food, prayers, and hope.

Feeding Programs

Community feeding drives that restore dignity and ensure children and families have a nutritious meal.

Children with Disabilities

Sponsorships and social support for children living with disabilities — academic fees, school materials, and community inclusion.

Youth Empowerment

Mentorship, skills training and guidance to help young people rise and lead change in their communities.

Farmers Support

We support smallholder farmers with training, improved techniques, resources, and market linkages so they can produce more and earn better livelihoods.

SMEs Empowerment

Small business training and mentorship, helping entrepreneurs build resilient micro-businesses that create local jobs and incomes.
